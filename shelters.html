Category 4 - Hurricane Ian

Hurricane shelters should be located on high ground, away from coastal areas and flood zones, to avoid storm surge and flooding. Sturdy, reinforced structures, such as schools, community centers, or concrete buildings, are ideal. They should be centrally located near population centers and evacuation routes for easy access. Home shelters must be reinforced interior spaces above flood levels, while public shelters should meet wind and flood safety standards. Accessibility, ventilation, and sufficient space are essential for safety and comfort.
